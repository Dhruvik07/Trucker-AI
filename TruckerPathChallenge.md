A dispatcher has to call or text each driver to check their location and
remaining HOS hours before assigning a load — with no system to compare
options across the fleet, they often put the wrong driver on a load,
creating costly deadhead miles and ripple effects across tomorrow's
assignments.

Loads in transit go dark — breakdowns, weather delays, and route
deviations aren't detected until a customer calls asking where their freight
is, and by then it's too late to reroute or relay.

After every load, the dispatcher manually chases drivers for BOLs, PODs,
fuel receipts, and rate confirmations, then manually reconciles them into
invoices — a process that delays cash flow by days and regularly results in
missed charges or billing errors.
